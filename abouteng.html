About Botanical Colour - The Redpath way

Led by artist Abi Makepeace and developed by Makepeace Studio CIC, Botanical Colour: The Redpath Way is a community heritage project celebrating the life and extraordinary work of Margaret and David Redpath – natural dye pioneers and the last owners of Wallis Woollen Mill in Ambleston, Pembrokeshire, Wales.

In their working life, Margaret and David developed over 160 dye recipes through their company Renaissance Dyeing, producing vivid, lasting colours for projects as renowned as the restoration of the Bayeux Tapestry and linen woven for Shakespeare's bedroom. They were widely regarded as world-leading authorities in natural dyeing and played a significant role in Welsh textile heritage, generously sharing knowledge with the international dye community throughout their lives.

Driven by deep social and environmental values, the Redpaths approached their craft with resourcefulness, respect for materials, and a spirit of genuine innovation – guided throughout by their Quaker faith. Their guiding principle of following the path of least resistance remains an inspiration in how we work and how we have come together throughout this project.

The project was delivered over fifteen months and involved deep research into the Redpaths' remarkable archive of dye recipes, hands-on natural dye workshops, the development of a community dye garden, and the creation of these 189 swatch experiments by project volunteers.

Thanks to National Lottery players, we have been able to share and preserve the cultural contributions of Margaret and David Redpath.

The National Lottery Heritage Fund is the largest funder for the UK’s heritage. Using money raised by National Lottery players we support projects that connect people and communities to heritage. Our vision is for heritage to be valued, cared for and sustained for everyone, now and in the future. From historic buildings, industrial legacy, natural environment, collections, traditions, stories and more, heritage can be anything from the past that people value and want to pass on to future generations. We believe in the power of heritage to ignite the imagination, offer joy and inspiration, and to build pride in place and connection to the past.
