Botanical Colour, The Redpath Way

Botanical Colour: The Redpath Way is a community heritage project led by artist Abi Makepeace and developed by Makepeace Studio CIC. The project celebrates the life and legacy of Margaret and David Redpath, natural dye pioneers and the last owners of Wallis Woollen Mill in Ambleston, Pembrokeshire.

Through their company Renaissance Dyeing, the Redpaths developed over 160 natural dye recipes and became internationally recognised for their expertise, contributing to projects including the restoration of the Bayeux Tapestry. Their work was guided by strong social and environmental values, rooted in resourcefulness, knowledge-sharing, and innovation.

Driven by deep social and environmental principles, Margaret and David approached their craft with a spirit of experimentation and care for materials, supported by a commitment to sharing knowledge with the wider dyeing community.

Over fifteen months, the project brought together archival research, natural dye workshops, a community dye garden, and the creation of 189 experimental swatches by volunteers.

All recipes featured on this website are licensed under a Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International (CC BY 4.0) licence. They may be freely used and shared, provided the project is appropriately credited.

The project archive, including colour swatches, notebook entries, research materials, and photographs created by Margaret and David throughout their lives, has been preserved through People's Collection Wales and the National Library of Wales. Learning resources developed during the project are also available as part of this growing legacy.

The National Lottery Heritage Fund is the largest funder for the UK’s heritage. Using money raised by National Lottery players we support projects that connect people and communities to heritage. Our vision is for heritage to be valued, cared for and sustained for everyone, now and in the future. From historic buildings, industrial legacy, natural environment, collections, traditions, stories and more, heritage can be anything from the past that people value and want to pass on to future generations. We believe in the power of heritage to ignite the imagination, offer joy and inspiration, and to build pride in place and connection to the past.
